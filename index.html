HOW I CAME OUT OF CULT AND INTO CHURCH .

  • I got initiated into cult, Vikings Fraternity, before I secured admission to study Accountancy at the Akanu Ibiam Federal Polytechnic. I became actively involved into it during my second semester in year one. But, something in me was telling me to consider my way. At a point I hated what I was doing, but didn’t know how to come out of it. However, after my OND, I decided to further my education at the Federal Polytechnic, Nekede, Owerri, Imo State. I went to Nekede desiring to create a new beginning. My reason of going to Federal Polytechnic, Nekede, was aborted because the Greater One wasn’t in me. Friends, cultism is a sinker of destiny. After one month in Nekede, I identified with the group again. I later realized that change of location does not equal to creating a new. Any change that does not begin with receiving Jesus into one’s life will amount to nothing. To worsen my case, I was a full time marijuana student at that time. I wasn’t just an addicted hemp smoker, but a “feared dealer.’’ One day I went to a primary school to smoke without knowing that the Police was tasked to arrest hoodlums who were terrorizing that location. That was on a Saturday morning. When I got to the premises, I looked but didn’t see anyone. But all of a sudden an armed police officer showed up from nowhere. This incident happened in my final year. Thanks be to Jesus Who never allowed me to go to hell. The Police officer searched my thoroughly, and found a sachet of hemp in my pocket. I pleaded with him, but he refused. The man insisted of taking me to the police station. On our way to the police station, he tried to bring down his gun which he hung on his shoulder. I pushed him and ran, and the officer brought out his gun and shut directly at me. No single bullet touched. On my way back to school, I saw a man who was holding a book written by Bishop David Abioye of Winners Chapel, “Creating A New Beginning”. The reader was beside me. TESTIMONY OF RICHARD WURMBRAND

I was an atheist, but atheism did not give me peace to my heart…During this time of inner turmoil, an old carpenter in a village high up in the mountains of Romania prayed like this:” My God, I have served You on earth and I wish to have my reward on earth as well as in heaven. And my reward should be that I should not die before I bring a Jew to Christ, because Jesus was from the Jewish people. But I am poor, old, and sick. I cannot go around and seek a Jew. In my village there are none. Bring a Jew into my village and I will do my best to bring him to Christ.” Something irresistible drew me to that village. I had no reason to go there. Romania has twelve thousand villages, but I went to that one. Seeing I was a Jew, the carpenter courted me as never a beautiful girl had been courted. He saw in me the answer to his prayer and gave me a Bible to read. I had read the Bible out of cultural interest many times before. But the Bible he gave me was another kind of Bible. As he told me sometime later, he and his wife prayed together for hours for my conversion and that of my wife. The Bible he gave was written not so much in words, but in flames of love fired by his prayers. I could barely read it. I could only weep over it, comparing my bad life with the life of Jesus; ...-and He accepted me as one of His own …Soon thereafter, my wife was converted. She brought other souls to Christ. Those other souls still brought still more souls to Christ… In 1939 my wife and I accepted another pastorate and moved away. But we went to that area where we had first lived to visit my wife’s parents. We went with them to visit a neighboring farmer. He had been sickly, but they hadn’t discovered exactly what was wrong. They knew it was serious. The women were in the house. This farmer, my father-in-law, and I were sitting on the front porch, talking. On the inside of me, I had a burden, an agony really, to pray for this farmer. As he and my father-in-law talked, the man opened the door by something he said so I could move right in and say something to him about the Lord. But I didn’t. I held my peace. Then the conversation changed. And I never could work it around again. I said nothing to him about his spiritual welfare. We spent another day or two with my wife’s folks and then came home. A couple of days later, mother-in-law called and said this man had died. I regretted for a while that I hadn’t followed up on the leading of the Lord and said nothing to him. Then I thought no more about it. Sunday night, as was our custom, we met at the altar to pray 15 minutes before the service. I stepped off the platform, knelt, and had just shut my eyes when I was in the Spirit. I saw that man. I shall never forget it. It took me weeks to get away from it. I would wake up in the night and see it. I saw this man in hell. I saw the fires of hell boiling up around him. I heard his screams. I heard him cried out like the rich man did for water to cool his tongue. Then I saw Jesus. He pointed at me and said,” I’m going to hold you responsible for him. I gave you a burden of prayer, and you didn’t pray. I opened the door for you to talk, and you didn’t talk.” “Oh, my God! I cried. With tears, on my knees for an hour and half, I repented crying,” Oh, God. Let thing pass from me.” I didn’t preach that night. The others didn’t know what was happening.

How to Pray Cultists Out of Cult And Into Church

People often get discouraged when praying for the salvation of loved ones and begin to wonder if their prayers ever really help. Well, guess what? Yes! Your prayers can be a powerful force in the lives of your unsaved relatives and friends if you know how to pray for them according to the wisdom and Word of God. The problem is that most Christians don’t know how to pray with skill. They just throw prayers in every direction and hope something comes of it. That’s especially true when it comes to praying for the lost. It’s not uncommon to see believers praying for friends or loved ones to get saved for 20, 30 or even 40 years without results. What exactly are these people praying? It usually boils down to something like this: “Oh, God, please save my brother. He needs to be saved.” (Don’t you think God knows he needs saving?) Then they might go a step further and start rehearsing their brother’s sins: “Lord, he got drunk again last night and we had to bail him out of jail again. If You don’t save him, he’s going to drive us all crazy!” That may sound funny—the example is a little extreme to make a point—but many of us have prayed prayers that sounded something like that. What we haven’t understood is this: There’s nothing wrong with the cultists you see in the campus (or any other unsaved person) except the devil. No human being who truly understood the salvation Jesus has offered to us would reject that salvation of his own free will. No one! Why, then, are so many people still joining cult after seeing how students are massacred regularly? II Corinthians 4:4 tells us it is because “the god of this world [the devil] hath blinded the minds of them which believe not, lest the light of the glorious gospel of Christ, who is the image of God, should shine unto them.” Once you realize it’s the devil (not the student ) who’s the real problem, your first prayer step becomes clear. You must get the devil out of every cultist to get them saved. From my experience, and others who were once cultists, but are serving the Lord today, every cultist is originally created by God to proclaim the Gospel. As Jesus said, “How can one enter into a strong man’s house, and spoil his goods, except he first bind the strong man?” (Matthew 12:29). You can’t! So bind him. Say, “You spirit operating in the life of this student,(-name ---------), blinding him/her to the gospel to keep him/her out of the kingdom of God, I bind you now. I belong to the Lord Jesus Christ. I carry His authority and righteousness, and in His Name I command you to desist in your maneuvers. I spoil your house according to the Word of God and I enter into it to deliver this student (mention the person’s name) one from your hands.” Now, you may not be able to cast that devil entirely out of the situation, because the student may invite him back in faster than you can cast him out. But you can bind that spirit and keep it bound. It may keep you busy for a while, but you can do it. You’ll find your next prayer step in Matthew 9:37-38. There, Jesus was looking out at the multitudes who needed ministry and He said to His disciples, “The harvest truly is plenteous, but the laborers are few; pray ye therefore the Lord of the harvest, that he will send forth laborers into his harvest.” It’s the Word of God that brings faith for salvation: “Faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God” (Romans 10:17). What finally convicted and converted me was the word of God in 1Cor2 :9” But as it is written, Eye hath not seen, nor ear heard, neither have entered into the heart of man, the things which God hath prepared for them that love him”. So you need to see to it that the Word gets into the heart of these prospective kingdom brethren “How?” you say, “These students won’t listen to me!” Probably not, but God knows who these students will listen to, and He’ll send that person halfway around the world to talk to these students if necessary. So pray to the Lord of the harvest: “Father, I’m asking You to send laborers across ’s path, laborers who are equipped with the Word of God about salvation, the Baptism in the Holy Spirit, healing and deliverance. Send laborers who can communicate effectively with so Your Word can reach his/her heart.” Once you’ve prayed these students like that, from then on, put your faith in action by treating these students like they are saved. Don’t treat these students like they are not good. On the contrary, every time you see them, say, “Praise God! Isn’t it good to know Jesus?” Then just stand back and watch how things change. You’ll be amazed. It’s happened many times. And it can happen that way for you and your loved one. So don’t give up. Get smart! Start praying according to the Word and praying by faith…and you will see the salvation of the Lord!
